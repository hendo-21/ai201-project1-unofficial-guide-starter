## Myles Standish Hall > The History of Myles Standish Hall
Myles Standish Hall has been an integral part of the Boston University (BU) campus since its construction in 1949. Named after Mayflower passenger Myles Standish, the dorm was designed in the International Style by renowned architect Paul Rudolph.
Originally constructed as a men's-only residence hall, Myles Standish became co-ed in 1971 and has remained so ever since. Over the decades, it has housed countless BU students from all walks of life, contributing to the diverse, vibrant community that defines campus culture.
Today, Myles Standish Hall stands as a Boston University landmark, its distinctive 16-story tower visible from all around Comm Ave. As one of the largest dorms on campus, it continues to be a hub of activity, hosting student events, study sessions, and late-night snack runs in its bustling common areas.

## Myles Standish Hall > Amenities & Facilities
As one of BU's largest and most prominent residence halls, Myles Standish is equipped with a wide array of amenities and facilities to support student life. Some of the key features include:
Dorm Rooms: Myles offers a variety of room types, including singles, doubles, triples, and quads. All rooms come furnished with beds, desks, dressers, and closet space.
Communal Lounges: There are multiple common lounge areas throughout the building for studying, socializing, and relaxing. Many have TVs, pool tables, and other recreational equipment.
Laundry Facilities: Myles has multiple laundry rooms with washers and dryers available for resident use.
Dining Options: Just downstairs in the Myles Standish basement is a Dunkin' Donuts, providing a convenient caffeine and snack fix. The larger Warren Towers Dining Hall is also located right next door.
Fitness Center: Myles residents have access to a small fitness center with basic workout equipment.
Security: The dorm has 24/7 security, with card-swipe access and staff monitoring the entrances.
While the facilities and amenities in Myles are certainly impressive, keep in mind that as a large, high-traffic dorm, it can sometimes feel crowded or noisy, especially in common areas. Managing your time and finding quiet study spots may take some creativity.

## Myles Standish Hall > Room Types & Layouts
When it comes to room options, Myles Standish Hall offers a variety of configurations to suit different living preferences and group sizes:
Singles: These private rooms provide a quiet, independent living experience, though they are the most limited in availability.
Doubles: The most common room type, doubles are shared between two residents and come furnished with two beds, desks, and dressers.
Triples: As the name implies, these rooms accommodate three students, with bunk beds to maximize the space.
Quads: The largest room option, quads house four residents, again with bunk beds to fit everything in.
Regardless of the configuration, all Myles Standish rooms include the basic furnishings - bed, desk, dresser, and closet space. You'll need to provide your own linens, decor, and any additional furniture or accessories.
Room assignments are determined through the general BU housing lottery process, so your specific room type and layout will depend on availability and your lottery number. But no matter where you end up in Myles, the close-knit community and prime location make it a coveted BU housing option.
