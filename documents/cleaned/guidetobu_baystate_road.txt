## Bay State Road > Welcome to Bay State Road: BU's Premier On-Campus Community
Bay State Road stretches along the Charles River just steps from central campus and houses a significant portion of Boston University (BU) students in a mix of dorms and residential buildings spanning several blocks of Commonwealth Avenue. The neighborhood has become the unofficial heart of undergraduate life at BU—a tightly clustered residential zone where most first-years and many upperclassmen eventually live during their time here.
Unlike scattered off-campus apartments across the city, Bay State Road keeps the housing community concentrated enough that you'll see classmates constantly, yet independent enough that it feels like your own corner of Boston. This guide walks through the actual dorm options, what daily life looks like there, and how the neighborhood functions beyond the official BU bubble.

## Bay State Road > Bay State Road Dorms: Your Home Away from Home
The heart of the Bay State Road experience lies in its iconic dorm complexes. As a BU student, you'll have the opportunity to live in one of several renowned residence halls that dot this picturesque stretch of Comm Ave.
Myles Standish Hall: One of the most recognizable Bay State dorms, Myles Standish is a high-rise building that houses predominantly upperclassmen. With suite-style living and sweeping views of the Charles River, it's a popular choice for juniors and seniors.
Kilachand Hall: Formerly known as the Shelton Hall, Kilachand is a contemporary residential complex that features apartment-style living and a variety of amenities, including a fitness center and communal lounges.
Claflin Hall: A classic brick building that exudes a cozy, close-knit vibe, Claflin is a great option for first-year students looking to fully immerse themselves in the Bay State community.
Additionally, the Warren Towers complex, which includes Warren, Silber, and Sleeper Halls, is located just off Bay State Road and is a popular choice for freshmen.

## Bay State Road > Amenities and Perks of Bay State Road Living
Living on Bay State Road comes with a host of convenient amenities and benefits that enhance the overall college experience:
Proximity to Campus: With the BU central campus just a short walk away, Bay State residents have easy access to classrooms, dining halls, and other university resources.
Green Spaces: The tree-lined streets and Charles River Esplanade provide ample opportunities for outdoor recreation, study sessions, and enjoying the beautiful Boston scenery.
Vibrant Off-Campus Community: Beyond the dorms, Bay State Road is home to a thriving off-campus community, with local shops, restaurants, and social hubs that cater to BU students.
Public Transportation: The MBTA Green Line's B branch runs directly through the Bay State area, making it a breeze to get around campus and explore the city.
Amenities and Resources: Many of the Bay State dorms offer on-site amenities like fitness centers, study lounges, and community kitchens to enhance the residential experience.
