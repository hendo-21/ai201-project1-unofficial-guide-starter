Myles is definitely one of the best dorms on campus. It is suite-style housing with suites of 4-8 and mixes of singles and doubles. The singles are adequately sized for one person and the doubles are generally quite spacious. If you're on the bay state rd side, on the top floors, you have a great view of the charles river. There is air conditioning and the windows can be opened, so heat is never a problem. The main downside is the walk to any classes in central or west campus. However, you're closer to fun stuff off-campus like Newbury St and Copley. Since I transferred to BU, I have been living in Myles. I love the suite-style experience mainly because I don't have to worry about sharing a bathroom with a dozen other people. Also, the dorms themselves are much newer compared to most of East Campus and come with semi-working air conditioners (personally, I never needed to bring a fan). There are several spots in the building where you can study or do work quietely. Only downside is that Myles is a slight far walk from Central Campus, usually taking me 10-15 minutes to get to classes when I don't take the bus.

[REVIEW | dorm=Myles Standish Hall | date=2022-01-01 | room_type=single]
The building is newly renovated and very nice. Size of the singles varies a bit; mine was big enough but my suitmate’s was definitely bigger. The AC couldn’t be lowered below 72, so I usually kept the windows open. No pest problems. Saw a single spider in my entire year living here. Otherwise, the amenities were nice. It was so convenient to have mail and a kitchen inside the building. The walk to Marciano dining hall isn’t too far, although the hike to classes is a bit worse than other dorms. Highly recommend Myles if you can get in. Easily the nicest dorm on East campus.
